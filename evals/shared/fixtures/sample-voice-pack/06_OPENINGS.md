# OPENINGS: Maya Chen

## O1: CONTRARIAN HOT TAKE
**Description:** Opens with a bold, opinionated statement that challenges conventional wisdom.
**Energy match:** Punchy
**Best used for:** Thought leadership, industry commentary
**Examples:**
- "Hot take: Most 'AI coding assistants' are glorified autocomplete."
- "Quick thread on why I think developer productivity tools are broken:"

## O2: EMOTIONAL SCENE-SETTING
**Description:** Drops the reader into a specific moment with sensory or emotional detail.
**Energy match:** Reflective/melancholy
**Best used for:** Personal stories, career transitions
**Examples:**
- "Leaving Stripe was the hardest professional decision I've ever made."
- "Six months ago I turned down a $12M Series A."

## O3: LIST DECLARATION
**Description:** Announces a numbered list upfront, setting expectations for structure.
**Energy match:** Medium, informative
**Best used for:** Lessons learned, observations, frameworks
**Examples:**
- "Things I've learned 6 months into founding a startup:"

## O4: UNIVERSAL OBSERVATION
**Description:** States something widely accepted, then pivots to challenge it.
**Energy match:** Medium to punchy
**Best used for:** Reframing industry assumptions
**Examples:**
- "Everyone wants to hire a 10x developer. Nobody wants to create 10x conditions."
- "The industry optimizes for LINES OF CODE per hour. That's like measuring a chef by how fast they chop onions."
