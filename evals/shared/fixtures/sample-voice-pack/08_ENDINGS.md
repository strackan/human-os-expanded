# ENDINGS: Maya Chen

## E1: WRY PARENTHETICAL
**Description:** Closes with a parenthetical aside that's funny, self-aware, or both. Lightens serious content.
**Pairs with:** O1+M2, O4+M4
**Best used for:** Thought leadership (leaves reader smiling)
**Examples:**
- "(Yes, I'm biased. But I'm also right.)"
- "(This is also why our pricing model is different but that's a post for another day)"

## E2: CALLBACK
**Description:** References something from the opening or a detail mentioned earlier. Creates narrative closure.
**Pairs with:** O2+M1, O2+M5
**Best used for:** Personal stories, emotional posts
**Examples:**
- "She texted back a heart emoji. We're good."
- "That weekend project became FlowState."

## E3: SIMPLE TRUTH
**Description:** Ends with a short, declarative statement that lands like a mic drop. No elaboration.
**Pairs with:** O1+M2, O4+M4
**Best used for:** Contrarian takes, strong opinion pieces
**Examples:**
- "I haven't regretted it once."
- "The money isn't the constraint. The speed is."

## E4: OPEN INVITATION
**Description:** Closes by acknowledging the reader's experience or inviting reflection without a direct question.
**Pairs with:** O2+M5, O4+M2
**Best used for:** Posts about universal experiences
**Examples:**
- "I'm not saying everyone should quit their job. But if you've been having That Feeling for more than 6 months, it might be time to listen to it."

## E5: META ACKNOWLEDGMENT
**Description:** Steps back to acknowledge the format, the post itself, or the irony of the situation.
**Pairs with:** Any O+M combination
**Best used for:** Longer posts, especially when the topic is earnest
**Examples:**
- "(And yes, my mom was concerned about the smaller number. But she sent a heart emoji when I explained. We're good.)"
