# Daily Review Mode

## Purpose
End-of-day reflection on wins, challenges, and lessons.
Helps close the day with intention and prepare for tomorrow.

## Review Structure

### 1. Wins & Highlights
"What was the highlight of your day?"
- What went well?
- What are you proud of?
- What unexpected good happened?

### 2. Challenges & Learning
"What challenged you today?"
- What was difficult?
- What didn't go as planned?
- What would you do differently?

### 3. Energy Check
"How are your energy levels?"
- Physical energy (1-10)
- Mental energy (1-10)
- Emotional energy (1-10)

### 4. Gratitude Moment
"What's one thing you're grateful for from today?"
- A person who helped
- A moment of joy
- Something that worked out

### 5. Tomorrow Preview
"What will you carry forward to tomorrow?"
- One priority for tomorrow
- Something to let go of
- An intention to set

## Quick Format (5 minutes)

```
WINS:
-

CHALLENGES:
-

GRATEFUL FOR:
-

TOMORROW:
-
```

## Extended Format (15 minutes)

### Morning Intentions vs. Reality
- What did you intend to do today?
- What actually happened?
- What accounts for the difference?

### People Interactions
- Who did you connect with today?
- How did those interactions go?
- Any follow-ups needed?

### Progress Check
- What moved forward today?
- What's still stuck?
- What needs attention?

### Emotional Journey
- How did your mood evolve through the day?
- Any patterns to note?
- What affected your state?

### Lessons Learned
- What did today teach you?
- Any insights or realizations?
- What will you remember?

## Entity Linking
- Link people mentioned in wins/challenges
- Tag tasks and projects referenced
- Note relationships affected by the day
- Create action items for follow-ups

## Mood Tracking
- Capture overall day mood
- Note any significant shifts
- Track energy patterns
- Watch for burnout signals

## Closing Ritual
"Before closing the day..."
- Release anything you're holding onto
- Appreciate yourself for getting through
- Set an intention for rest
- Look forward to tomorrow

## Tips
- Keep it honest, not performative
- Bad days count too
- Notice patterns over time
- Use this data to adjust
